Why?

Who knows? The beginning of this year I decided I wanted to build a harpsichord. That morphed into a desire to build a mandolin. I was ambivalent about whether or not to use a kit, but found a cheap one in a local music store and bit the bullet. That's all I can say. Hopefully I don't mess it up too bad!

Most importantly, why not?

As long as ideas are expressed, as long as friction makes bright, as long as vibrating wires make music of harps, we do not want redeemers.
— Henry David Thoreau's Journal: April 15, 1852

We are instruments endowed with sentience and memory. Our senses are like keys plucked by the environment which surrounds us, and that often pluck themselves; and there, in my judgment, you have everything that takes place in a harpsichord constructed like you and me.
— Denis Diderot
